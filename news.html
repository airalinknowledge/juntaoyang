2024/8/11
Within the framework of The 2nd Shanghai Museum/Gallery Staff Biennale, I had the honour to participate in the group show, Conflicts Within and Without, at SNAP, Shanghai, China. The exhibition not only refers to global geopolitical conflicts and natural disasters, but also to the internal psychological and physical struggles of human beings. The exhibition runs until 12 August.

The 2nd Shanghai Museum/Gallery Staff Biennale aims to organise art industry workers across institutions, forming a new exhibition entity out of the forces and social relations that make up the torrent, and developing an alternative mechanism between contemporary art institutions and workers. The biennale will consist of a pool of artworks submitted by the artists, from which the curators will select pieces to form a proposal, and the artists, the exhibition proposers, the exhibition planning team, and the host organisation will consult with each other to decide on the implementation plan, and then plan the exhibition ‘from the bottom up’. During the period of the biennale, exhibitions are held one after another, and artists and curators move to several exhibitions at the same time. The biennale is intended to form a temporary or continuous community of the participants.

Light and Cold Conversation: Atlantic, Algorithms and Visibility receives an Honorable Mention at Varsity Film Expo. The award, presented in Nairobi, Kenya, is described by the jury as "...serves as a powerful commentary on the complex relationship between technology, race, justice, and representation. The film's focus on digital representation and the politics of images connects to the discussions around digital inclusion and access to technology. The film raises questions about the ethical implications of AI and its potential to exacerbate existing inequalities and biases. The film encourages critical thinking about technology and its role in society, prompting viewers to consider the broader society and environmental impacts of technological advancement...." The film is also recommended to the UNESCO Sustainable Development Community as part of the honour.
