This page contains recent updates and fragments of my writing. These pieces may be temporary, unconsidered, and informal—unpublished now, and perhaps forever.
A Defense of Obscurity: Translating Judith Butler
September 27, 2025
In 2003, Judith Butler published a long essay titled "The Value of Difficulty." In this piece, she aimed to defend "obscure" and "unclear" language, a stance that continues to provoke debate. I further argue for the value, that difficulty in language is not mere obscurity but an ethical and political force that resists hegemonic clarity and continually renews critical thought.
A Radicalism That Is Empty: Memetic Politics and The Death of Charlie Kirk
September 13, 2025
The murderer lacked any clear political motive; his memetic declaration merely displayed a form of radicalism hollow at its core—an empty radicalism that emerges precisely as a consequence of a post-political neoliberal society.

Desiring Fascism, Sterile Aesthetics
September 20, 2025
Building on the debate between Susan Sontag and Adrienne Rich, this essay explores fascism's libidinal economy and proposes a counter-strategy: an art of exhaustion, enervation, and sterile aesthetics.

September 2, 2025
This essay examines the stigmatization of 'Typhoid Mary,' exploring how prejudice and power articulate themselves around a supposed truth, drawing a parallel to the film *Malèna*.

August 25, 2025
The debate over whether Lu Xun’s mural should preserve his smoking image points to the historical complexity of how reactionary and revolutionary forces have aesthetically coded the body and desire.
Rethinking René Wellek: On Destroying Literary Studies (1983)
August 9, 2025
René Wellek's 1983 essay “Destroying Literary Studies” critiqued the rise of post-structuralism and deconstruction. This essay re-examines his arguments, their blind spots, and their lasting influence.
